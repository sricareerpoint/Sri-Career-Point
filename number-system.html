What is Number System?

A Number System is a method of representing numbers. It is an important topic for SSC CHSL examinations.

Types of Numbers

1. Natural Numbers

Counting numbers are called Natural Numbers.

Example: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5...

2. Whole Numbers

Natural numbers including zero are called Whole Numbers.

Example: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4...

3. Integers

Positive numbers, negative numbers and zero are called Integers.

Example: -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3

4. Even Numbers

Numbers divisible by 2 are called Even Numbers.

Example: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10

5. Odd Numbers

Numbers not divisible by 2 are called Odd Numbers.

Example: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9

6. Prime Numbers

A number having exactly two factors, 1 and itself.

Example: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11

7. Composite Numbers

A number having more than two factors.

Example: 4, 6, 8, 9, 10

Unit Digit / Last Digit

Important Cyclicity

2: 2, 4, 8, 6 → Cycle = 4

3: 3, 9, 7, 1 → Cycle = 4

4: 4, 6 → Cycle = 2

5: Always 5

6: Always 6

7: 7, 9, 3, 1 → Cycle = 4

8: 8, 4, 2, 6 → Cycle = 4

9: 9, 1 → Cycle = 2

Shortcut Example

Find the unit digit of 215.

15 ÷ 4 gives remainder 3.

2 cycle: 2, 4, 8, 6

Answer = 8

Factors and Multiples

Factors

A factor divides a number exactly without leaving a remainder.

Example: Factors of 12 = 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

Multiples

Multiples are obtained by multiplying a number by 1, 2, 3...

Example: Multiples of 5 = 5, 10, 15, 20, 25...

HCF and LCM

HCF (Highest Common Factor)

The greatest common factor of two or more numbers.

Example: HCF of 12 and 18 = 6

LCM (Least Common Multiple)

The smallest common multiple of two or more numbers.

Example: LCM of 4 and 6 = 12

Important Formula ⭐

HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers

Example: For 12 and 18:

HCF = 6 and LCM = 36

6 × 36 = 12 × 18 = 216

Trailing Zeros in Factorial

Trailing zeros ante number ending lo unna zeros.

Example: 12000 has 3 trailing zeros.

Formula

Number of zeros in n! = n/5 + n/25 + n/125 + ...

Example: Find zeros in 120!

120 ÷ 5 = 24

120 ÷ 25 = 4

120 ÷ 125 = 0

Total = 24 + 4 = 28

Answer = 28 trailing zeros
